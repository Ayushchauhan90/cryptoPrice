ABOUT BITCOIN (BTC)

What is Bitcoin?

Bitcoin, the world’s first decentralized virtual currency, is the largest cryptocurrency globally in terms of market capitalization. Due to its decentra lized nature, Bitcoin acts as money or any other form of payment and is swapped between two parties without the intervention of any third party. Bitcoin was first described in a whitepaper in 2008 by an anonymous person (s) known as Satoshi Nakamoto. Later,

it was launched in January 2009. Bitcoin enables secure peer-to-peer (P2P) transactions on the internet; this means that all transactions happen directly without the need for any intermediary or third party to permit or facilitate them.

Who introduced Bitcoin?

Bitcoin was founded by an anonymous person (s), Satoshi Nakamoto. As of 2022, the true identity of Satoshi Nakamoto remains unknown. Bitcoin transactions are confirmed through telecommunication network nodes through cryptography and are later recorded in a decentralized distributed ledger called the blockchain.

What is the maximum Bitcoin circulation limit?

Bitcoin has a confined supply of 21 million coins. This means that the number of Bitcoins that can be issued is expected never to reach 21 million as its software limits them. No further bitcoins will be generated when the Bitcoin supply meets its upper limit. New coins are created during the process called mining, as transactions are relayed across the network, they are picked up by miners and packaged into batches called blocks, which are secured by complex cryptographic calculations. These miners receive rewards as compensation for spending their computational resources for every block that they have added to the blockchain. At the time Bitcoin was launched, the reward constituted 50 bitcoins/block. This figure gets halved with every 210,000 new blocks mined — which roughly takes the network four Years. Since the last halving in May 2020, successful miners have been rewarded with 6.25 bitcoins for each block

discovered. Bitcoin was not premined, meaning no coins were mined or distributed between the founders before it was made available to the public. However, during the initial years of BTC’s launch, the competition between miners was comparatively low, enabling the earliest network participants to gather significant amounts of coins through regular mining: it is believed that Satoshi Nakamoto alone owns over a million Bitcoin. Bitcoin mining can be very profitable, depending on Bitcoin price and the current hash rate. While the process of mining Bitcoins is complex, it is impossible to set out to mine exactly one Bitcoin in a given timeframe. New Bitcoin is minted each time a new block is validated on the Bitcoin blockchain.

How is the Bitcoin (BTC) Network Secured?

Bitcoin is secured with the Secure Hashing Algorithm (SHA) -256, which belongs to the SHA-2 family of cryptographic hashes. The protocol is also used by its fork Bitcoin Cash (BCH), and several other cryptocurrencies.

Bitcoin and Proof of Work (PoW) consensus mechanism:

Bitcoin is known for introducing the concept of proof of work (PoW) consensus mechanism in cryptocurrency. A PoW is a form of adding new blocks of transactions to Bitcoin’s blockchain. The work here is generating a hash matching the target hash for the current block. The crypto miners who perform this task get the right to add that block to the blockchain and receive rewards. Though Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies for security use PoW, the method is energy intensive and has a negative impact on the environment. The proof-of-work model is used to confirm and record crypto transactions

When Bitcoin transactions happen, they undergo security verification and are grouped into a block to be mined. Following this, Bitcoin’s PoW algorithm generates a hash for the block. As discussed earlier, Bitcoin uses the SHA-256 algorithm – and it always produces hashes with 64 characters.

Miners compete to be the first in the race to generate a target hash that is below the block hash. The winner obtains the right to add the latest block of transactions to the blockchain of Bitcoin. As compensation for their work, they also receive Bitcoin rewards as newly minted coins and transaction fees.
